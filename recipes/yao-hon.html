Yao Hon (Cambodian Hot Pot)

Description:

Yao Hon (ya-hawn) is a Cambodian Hot Pot dish that is packed with a variety of flavors: umami, spicy, tangy. It's one of my favorites due to the flavor profile that makes it stand out from every other hot pot. Hot pot is a communal way of eating. There's typically a large pot in the middle on a burner, and a plethora of different meats and vegetables around it. Participants would choose what they wanted to eat and add it to the pot.

There's no one way to make hot pot, as there is no one way to make Yao Hon. It's an easy dish to prepare, great for gatherings and cold nights. It's mostly prep work and less actual cooking, making this an easy recipe. This recipe is good for a large shallow pot and can feed between 3-6 people. You can always top-off the soup and buy additional meats and vegetables.

Ingredients:

Broth:

  • 2 8.5oz cans of Head Hot & Spicy Barbque Sauce (or non spicy if you prefer).
  • 2 cans of coconut milk
  • 1.5 cans of coconut soda (Coco Rico is the go-to) or 1/2 bottle of lager (Heineken is what my mom used, otherwise Modelo works too)
  • 1/2 cup of Soya Bean Curd Sauce
  • 2 tbsp of fish sauce
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • Set aside another 2 to 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon of palm sugar or normal sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/3 cup of crushed toasted peanuts (optional)

Add ins:

Note that these are suggestions! You can always pick and choose to your own preferences. It's best to keep cuts thin for quick and easy cooking.

  • Carbs:Rice noodles (i.e. Vermicelli, udon, glass noodles)
  • Meats: pork, lamb, beef
  • Seafood: shrimp, squid, clams, muscles
  • Vegetables: watercress, enoki mushrooms, bok choy, chinese broccoli, lotus root, cilantro
  • Other add-ins: quail eggs, fish balls, tofu, dumplings, meatballs, rice cakes

Directions:

  1. Prepare all add ins. Wash and cut all add ins and set aside. If you get pre-thinly sliced meat, keep frozen till ready to serve.
  2. Set aside noodles. You can add them into the pot or cook seperately. Follow instructions on back of packaging.
  3. In a pot, add coconut milk and bring to a light boil on medium-high heat, stiring often to prevent burning.
  4. Add the barbecue sauce and stir to combine.
  5. Then add Sorya Bean Curd Sauce, coconut soda, and chicken broth. Stir to combine and bring to a light boil.
  6. Add fish sauce and sugar, then adjust to your taste. Lightly simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Add crushed peanuts if using.
  8. In another pot, heat up 2 cups of chicken broth in a seperate pot and bring to a boil. Once it boils, turn off flame and cover. Add to hot pot when needed.

How to Cook and Eat:

Set the pot on a cooking element. Place add-in around the pot and within equidistant reach of everyone. Once it begins to slightly boil, add items that take the longes to cook. That's things like lots roots and fish balls. Then add in other meats and vegetables. Be careful with beef, it tends to cook very quick! Add in small amounts, we don't want to overcrowd the pot. Remove when they are cooked to your liking and enjoy!
